01 · Problem

AI output's worst enemy is re-inventing every time

Ask ChatGPT or Claude "write me a report" cold, and every run looks different — different colors, different structure, different voice. Style drift means AI output never compounds into an asset, let alone a personal identity.

The problem isn't that the AI is weak; it's that nothing constrains the AI's freedom. Every conversation is a blank page, so the model re-invents wheels — picking colors, picking fonts, picking section orders, picking tone. Even if you know in your head "I want the same kind as last time," without codified rules the AI starts over.

02 · Design

Design system as the single source of visual truth

tokens.json, patterns.md, and components/ live at the repo root under design-system/, shared by every skill. Each skill only decides its own structure, tone, and section logic — never colors or fonts.

The coral accent wasn't picked randomly — it came from a weighted comparison across amber, coral, and lime, factoring in WCAG contrast ratios and a "tech bro aesthetic" penalty. The reasoning lives in tokens.json's top _meta.accent_rationale field. Change one color here and every skill's output shifts in sync.

Core rule
"Modifying tokens.json affects every skill's output" is written into the file's own header, so future-me doesn't accidentally drift it.
03 · Design

Skills must read a gold standard — no blank-canvas generation

Every skill's SKILL.md enforces reading at least one file from examples/ before generating. Blank-canvas = quality drops. Anchor on an example = consistency compounds.

Paired with progressive disclosure in three layers: the frontmatter handles trigger matching, the body holds the main flow, and references/ / examples/ are loaded on demand. SKILL.md stays under 500 lines — never stuffing all rules into the main prompt, which would dilute instructions and waste tokens.

Iteration
Promote satisfying outputs into examples/ as new gold standards; record concrete failure modes into anti-patterns.md. Every generation fine-tunes both ends.
04 · Design

Outputs themselves are the iteration signal

Every output ships with three artifacts: the main deliverable (index.html), source-notes.md (user's raw input, verbatim), and generation-log.md (decisions, which example was referenced, what was skipped, which anti-patterns were avoided).

This isn't metadata tacked onto reports — it's training data for future evolution. If output quality drifts later, the matching generation-log tells you what the original reasoning was. If the skill needs adjusting, scanning a stack of source-notes reveals "users actually feed this kind of input — the SKILL.md should optimize for that."

Takeaway

Good AI tooling is tooling that constrains the AI

Generation is commodity now — everyone has GPT or Claude. The quality gap comes from how much structure you impose on AI's freedom. Design systems constrain visuals; anti-patterns constrain content; examples constrain structure. The more specific the constraint, the more stable — and more "yours" — the output.

The core of claude-present isn't the three skills; it's a mechanism for AI to continuously self-align. Every output is a small validation of the system. Every anti-pattern is a precaution against future drift. This is what engineering discipline looks like when applied to AI content generation.
